Emerging Threat: Breeding Grounds for Terrorism

In 2012, the United States Army in Africa received reports claiming that Boko Haram influence was waning. Novametrics refuted the claims and predicted locations of future vulnerability.

Approach: Novametrics provided the Army Research Lab (ARL) with analysis of open-source data to:

  1. Independently assess the influence of the Boko Haram, and
  2. Identify areas increasingly vulnerable to their activities.

Novametrics' work continues under the scope of a Small Business Innovative Research (SBIR) Award from the Department of the Secretary of Defense.

1. Refuting the Claim

Using dynamic data visualization, Novametrics showed Boko Haram increasing their influence in Northwestern Nigeria. Press "Start" to begin the animation.

After the animation completes, scroll the translucent grey box (lower plot) left & right to show northward migration of conflicts and emergence of conflicts related to the Boko Haram (red dots)."

2. Predicting locations of terrorist growth

Using Novametrics “weak-signal analysis”, we identified the regions becoming vulnerable to Boko Haram. Our proprietary weak-signal analysis integrates and analyzes large numbers of social-cultural and environmental datasets in the Novametrics database to predict regions vulerable to growth of organized terrorism.

The three steps in this dynamic graphic:

  1. Loading data -- the highly variable (noisy) socio-cultural and environmental data show no clear pattern.
  2. Weak-signal analysis -- predictions emerge to reveal the states vulnerable to organized terrorism (brown).
  3. Verification -- Boko Haram conflicts (red dots) proliferate in vulerable regions (brown) and in Nigeria's capital.
The map shows a high-resolution analysis. Vulnerable regions (darker brown) exhibit conflicts attributed to Boko Haram. The widely publicized 2014 kidnapping of Nigerian girls occurred in northeastern Nigeria at a secondary school in the southern Borno.

3. Assessment proves correct.

Timeline:
  • 2012 -- Novametrics develops predictions for the United States African Command (AFRICOM).
  • Early 2013 -- The Office of the Secretary of Defense awards Novametrics a $1M SBIR for commercialization (see Figure, below left).
  • November 2013 -- The United States State Department designates Boko Haram as a terrorist group.

Three internationally recognized groups operate in Africa today: the radical Islamist Boko Haram operates in Nigeria, al Qaeda in Islamic Maghreb (AQIM) operates in Algeria and Mali, and al Shabaab has been plaguing Somalia and Kenya. These groups are rapidly expanding and, more alarmingly, claiming ties to al Qaeda in the Middle East.
