Impact Investing: Liberia Case Study

  • Streamlining the due-diligence process for successful business ventures.
  • Identifying prime investment opportunities and risk-mitigation strategies.

The Opportunity: Liberia’s natural resources, labor force, and pivotal leadership role in sub-Saharan Africa make it a nation of high priority for international development organizations and impact investment groups. The goal is to identify prime investment opportunities that will align with economic incentive programs and serve as flagships for highly-profitable, environmentally-sustainable, economic development ventures.

Approach: Novametrics' algorithms and extensive data archives were used to maximize profit, mitigate risk, and optimize impact.

Step 1: Maximizing impact and sustainability

The Novametrics database contains a full array of datasets for evaluating the socio-economic landscape. The dynamic graphic below presents a database selection relevant to the Liberia case study. Sort criteria provide different perspectives on the data:

  1. Random Order -- Source data are neither ranked nor sorted.
  2. Order by Group -- Novametrics categorizes and ranks indicators to address case-specific issues. Darker colors within each group represent higher ranking. .
  3. Order by Rank -- Ranked sort across all categories reveals impact opportunities (mouse over colored cells to identify categories).
The data analysis for Liberia, when sorted into groups, demonstrated strong labor potential and under-used agricultural resources.

Step 2: Predicting regions for investment

Using the previous selection criteria, our analysis optimized:

  1. Economic development: Wealth derived from Liberia's natural resources must promote local economic development.
  2. Environmental Sustainability & Stewardship: Natural resources must be used in an sustainable and carbon-netrural manner.
  3. Poverty reduction: Reduce poverty through creation of middle-class jobs.

Step 3: Identification of Prime Opportunity

The optimization analysis yielded 4 prime opportunities for value-added use of natural resources consistent with Liberia’s national priorities. In this case, the Buchanan location was selected because of its access to international transportation and local support for a latex-products manufacturing plant.
